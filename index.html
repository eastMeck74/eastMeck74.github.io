Looking back to 1974

A look at the year's biggest songs

February 8 – After a record 84 days in orbit, the crew of Skylab 4 returns to Earth

March 29 - The Terracotta Army of Qin Shi Huang is discovered at Xi'an, China

April 5 – Carrie, the debut novel by Stephen King, is published

April 6 – Swedish pop group ABBA's song Waterloo wins the 1974 Eurovision Song Contest in Brighton, England, UK

June 29 – Isabel Perón is sworn in as the first female President of Argentina, becoming one of the first female republican heads of state in the world, and the first woman to serve as president of a country

August 8 – U.S. President Richard Nixon announces his resignation on August 8, effective at noon on August 9

October 30 - George Foreman vs. Muhammad Ali, billed as The Rumble in the Jungle, took place in Kinshasa, Zaire (now Democratic Republic of the Congo)

November 24 – A skeleton from the hominid species Australopithecus afarensis is discovered and named Lucy

The Willis Tower (originally the Sears Tower), a 110-story, 1,451-foot (442.3 m) skyscraper, was completed

The Rubik's Cube, a 3-D combination puzzle, was invented by Hungarian sculptor and professor of architecture Ernő Rubik
